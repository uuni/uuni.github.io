Uuni is a beautiful collection of earthenware pots used for steam cooking in the oven. Uuni awards this traditional cooking method with a modern shape defined by simplicity, a tasteful brownish-red color and high multifunctional application that meet nowadays range of kitchen, cooking and society.

Uuni pots were Bronze awarded at the 10th International Ceramic Competition MINO in September 2014 and was exhibited during the festival in Tajimi,

Healthy cooking

The obsession in our society to speed up everyday tasks with fast-food and unhealthy lifestyles has provoked us to reverse this phenomenon by designing an oven pot for all those slow-food lovers and gourmands out there, who despite their busy schedule take time in preparing food with a healthy process and keeping ecological values in mind. The clay pot requires longer cooking time and lower temperature, which in return makes the food inside the clay pot preserve the nutrients and creates a tender flavor to your dish. That is what we call a "happy meal"!

Form and Material

Uuni pots are made from red earthenware and ash-wood. The materials can be taken from local resources and the manufacturing takes up little energy.

Uuni pots come in two sizes in order to meet demands from large families to single households, from traditional meals to modern and individual cooking and baking. When not used in the oven, red earthenware stays cold when soaked in water – pots work as well for storing cold dishes and raw- ingredients at room temperature. In addition, the ceramic or wooden lids can be used as plates, trivets or cutting boards. Small pots fit inside the big one, making it compact and easy to store.

Three designers -
one philosophy

Anna Kantanen, Lisa Keller and Tiina Leinonen love designing products that speak to one’s senses. Especially cooking and serving that deals with taste and quality, with getting together and sharing, with tradition and culinary fusion attracts them. The focus on table-top culture matched with paying attention to ecological and environmental issues is philosophy and red thread of the Finnish-German design trio.
