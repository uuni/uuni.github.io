UUNI

Design for Good Food

Internationally awarded

At MINO, Japan

Uuni is a beautiful collection of earthenware pots used for steam cooking in the oven. Uuni awards this traditional cooking method with a modern shape defined by simplicity, a tasteful brownish-red color and high multifunctional application that meet nowadays range of kitchens, cooking and lifestyles.

The Uuni pots were awarded Bronze at the 10th International Ceramic Competition MINO in September 2014 and exhibited during the festival in Tajimi, Japan.

Healthy cooking

The obsession to speed up everyday tasks leading to hasty lifestyles has provoked us to reverse this phenomenon by designing an oven pot for all slow-food lovers and gourmands, who despite their busy schedule take time in preparing food with a slower but healthier process. The pot requires longer cooking time and lower temperatures, which in return makes the food inside the clay pot preserve the nutrients, creating a tender flavour to your dish. That is what we call a "happy meal"!

Form and Material

The prototypes are realised using red earthenware, with additional lids of Ash-wood. The manufacturing is energy sparse due to only one firing and using no harmful glazes.

The pots come in two sizes in order to meet demands of large families to single households, from traditional meals to modern and individual cooking and baking.

When not used in the oven, red earthenware stays cold when soaked in cold water – therefore pots work well for storing dishes and raw ingredients at room temperature. In addition, the ceramic and wooden lids can be used as plates, trivets or cutting boards, and the small pots fit inside the big one making it compact and easy to store.
